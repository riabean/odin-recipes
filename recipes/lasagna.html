Lasagna della Nonna

Description

This cozy lasagna is the kind of meal that invites everyone to slow down and gather around the table. With layers of tender pasta, a rich tomato filling, and a beautifully golden top, it offers all the comfort of a classic baked dish while feeling nourishing and satisfying at the same time. It is the sort of recipe that works wonderfully for a quiet evening at home, but is also lovely enough to share with family or friends.

What makes this lasagna especially inviting is the balance of hearty and simple ingredients coming together in each layer. The sauce brings depth and warmth, the creamy elements soften every bite, and the baked top adds that irresistible finish that makes oven dishes so comforting. Served with a crisp salad or some warm bread on the side, it becomes a meal that feels both grounding and special.

Ingredients

For the meat sauce

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 500 g ground beef
  • 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 120 ml beef broth or water
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper

For the béchamel sauce

  • 50 g butter
  • 50 g all-purpose flour
  • 500 ml milk
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper

For the layers

  • 9–12 lasagna sheets
  • 200 g mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 60 g Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Fresh parsley or basil, chopped (for garnish)

Optional for serving

  • Extra Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • A drizzle of olive oil

Steps

  1. Prepare the meat sauce.

    Heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook for 3 to 4 minutes until softened. Stir in the garlic and cook for another 30 seconds.

  2. Brown the meat.

    Add the ground beef to the pan and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks.

  3. Build the sauce.

    Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, broth or water, oregano, basil, salt, and black pepper. Lower the heat and let the sauce simmer for 15 to 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until slightly thickened.
